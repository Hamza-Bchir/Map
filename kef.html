Introduction

The Governorate of Kef is one of the governorates of Tunisia, located in the northwestern part of the country. Known for its historical sites and cultural heritage, Kef is a region with a rich tapestry of traditions and landscapes.

Geography

Kef covers an area of approximately 5 081 square kilometers and is characterized by its hilly terrain. The governorate is known for its scenic landscapes and historical sites, making it a destination for both nature enthusiasts and history buffs.

Population

The population of the Governorate of Kef is around 243 156. Kef is home to a diverse community, and its cultural traditions are influenced by a mix of Arab and Berber heritage.

Culture

The culture of Kef is enriched by its historical significance. The governorate is known for its festivals, traditional crafts, and vibrant cultural events that celebrate the unique heritage of the region.

Zaouia

Sidi Bou Makhlouf, whose real name is Abdallah Bou Makhlouf El Gzouni, is a Tunisian saint and the patron saint of the city of Kef. He belongs to the Aïssawa Sufi order founded in Meknes in the 16th century. Following his death, a zaouïa was established in his honor.

Introduction

The Governorate of Zaghouan is one of the governorates of Tunisia, situated in the northeast of the country. Known for its natural beauty, historical significance, and cultural heritage, Zaghouan is a region with diverse landscapes.

Geography

Zaghouan covers an area of approximately 2 820 square kilometers and is characterized by its hills, forests, and proximity to the Mediterranean Sea. The governorate is known for the Jebel Zaghouan mountain and its natural springs.

History

Zaghouan has a rich history, with influences from Phoenicians, Romans, and Arabs. The governorate is home to archaeological sites, including the Temple of Water and the Aqueduct, showcasing its historical and cultural significance.

Tourism

Zaghouan is a destination for nature lovers and history enthusiasts. The governorate offers opportunities for hiking in the Jebel Zaghouan mountain, exploring ancient ruins, and enjoying the picturesque landscapes and natural springs.

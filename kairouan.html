Introduction

The Governorate of Kairouan is one of the governorates of Tunisia, situated in the central part of the country. Renowned for its historical significance and cultural heritage, Kairouan is a city with a rich tapestry of traditions and landmarks.

Geography

Kairouan covers an area of approximately 6 712 square kilometers and is characterized by its inland location. The governorate is known for its arid landscapes and is an important cultural and religious center in Tunisia.

History

Kairouan has a storied history dating back to ancient times. It became a prominent center of Islamic civilization, known for its mosques, madrasas, and historical monuments. The city has played a vital role in shaping the cultural identity of Tunisia.

Culture

The culture of Kairouan is deeply rooted in Islamic traditions. The governorate is known for its traditional crafts, including carpet weaving and ceramics. Kairouan has a vibrant cultural scene, with various festivals and events celebrating its heritage.

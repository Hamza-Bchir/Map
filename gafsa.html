Introduction

The Governorate of Gafsa is one of the governorates of Tunisia, located in the southwestern part of the country. Known for its rich natural resources and historical significance, Gafsa is a region with a diverse cultural heritage.

Geography

Gafsa covers an area of approximately 7 807 square kilometers and is characterized by a mix of arid landscapes and oases. The governorate is known for its unique geological formations and scenic beauty.

Population

The population of the Governorate of Gafsa is around 337 331. Gafsa is home to a diverse community, and its cultural traditions are influenced by a mix of Arab, Berber, and other cultural elements.

Natural Resources

Gafsa is renowned for its abundant natural resources, including phosphates and minerals. The governorate plays a crucial role in Tunisia's economy due to its mining activities and the extraction of valuable minerals.

Famous People

Among Gafsa's famous people we mention Ismail Khalil born on July 11, 1932, in Gafsa and passed away on November 20, 2017, was a powerful Tunisian diplomat, politician, and economist.

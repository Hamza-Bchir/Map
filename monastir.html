Introduction

The Governorate of Monastir is one of the governorates of Tunisia, situated on the northeast coast of the country. Known for its historical significance and coastal beauty, Monastir is a region with a rich cultural heritage and economic importance.

Geography

Monastir covers an area of approximately 1 024 square kilometers and is characterized by its coastal landscapes along the Mediterranean Sea. The governorate is known for its sandy beaches, historic monuments, and the city of Monastir.

History

Monastir has a storied history, with influences from various civilizations, including the Phoenicians, Romans, and Arabs. The governorate is home to historical sites such as Ribat of Monastir and the Mausoleum of Habib Bourguiba.

Economy

Monastir's economy is diverse, with contributions from sectors such as tourism, agriculture, and manufacturing. The governorate's strategic location and historical attractions make it a popular destination for both local and international tourists.

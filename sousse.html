Introduction

The Governorate of Sousse is one of the governorates of Tunisia, located on the eastern coast of the country. Known for its historical significance and vibrant tourism, Sousse is a region with a rich cultural heritage and economic importance.

Geography

Sousse covers an area of approximately 2 669 square kilometers and is characterized by its coastal landscapes along the Mediterranean Sea. The governorate is known for its sandy beaches, historical sites, and the city of Sousse.

History

Sousse has a rich history, with influences from various civilizations, including the Phoenicians, Romans, and Arabs. The governorate is home to historical sites such as the Medina of Sousse, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Tourism

Sousse is a major tourist destination in Tunisia, attracting visitors with its beaches, resorts, and cultural attractions. The governorate's strategic location and historical significance make it a popular choice for both domestic and international tourists.

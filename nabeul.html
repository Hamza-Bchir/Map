Introduction

The Governorate of Nabeul is one of the governorates of Tunisia. It is located in the northwest of the country and has a population of around 550,000. It is famous for its beaches, mountains, and rich cultural heritage.

Geography

The governorate covers an area of approximately 2 840 square kilometers and is located in the western part of the country. It has a diverse landscape, ranging from sandy beaches to high mountains. Some of the main towns in the governorate include Nabeul, Hammamet, and Soliman.

Population

The governorate of Nabeul has a population of around 787 920. The majority of the population lives in urban areas, with cities like Nabeul and Hammamet being the most populous. The population is mainly composed of Tunisians and tourists visiting the governorate.

Tourism

The governorate of Nabeul is a popular tourist destination in Tunisia. It is known for its stunning beaches, such as those found in Hammamet and Soliman, as well as its diverse natural landscapes. Additionally, the governorate boasts a rich cultural heritage, with various archaeological sites and historical monuments.

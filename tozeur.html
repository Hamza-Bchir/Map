Introduction

The Governorate of Tozeur is one of the governorates of Tunisia, situated in the southwestern part of the country. Known for its oasis, Saharan landscapes, and historical sites, Tozeur is a region with a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.

Geography

Tozeur covers an area of approximately 5 593 square kilometers and is characterized by its desert landscapes and palm groves. The governorate is known for the Tozeur Oasis, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the Chott el Djerid salt pan.

Economy

Tozeur's economy is based on agriculture, particularly date farming, and tourism. The governorate's unique landscapes and cultural attractions make it a destination for tourists seeking to explore the Saharan region of Tunisia.

Tourism

Tozeur is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors with its Saharan landscapes, historic medina, and cultural festivals. The governorate offers unique experiences, including camel treks, visits to ancient ksour, and exploration of the Sahara Desert.

Famous People

Aboul-Qacem Echebbi was a Tunisian poet. He is probably best known for writing the final two verses of the current National Anthem of Tunisia, Humat al-Hima.

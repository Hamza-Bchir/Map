Introduction

The Governorate of Bizerte is one of the governorates of Tunisia. Situated in the northern part of the country, it has a population of approximately 568 219. Known for its natural beauty and historical significance, Bizerte attracts both locals and tourists.

Geography

The governorate covers an area of about 3750 square kilometers and is located in the northernmost part of Tunisia. It features a diverse landscape, including coastal areas, mountains, and plains. Key towns in the governorate include Bizerte, Menzel Bourguiba, and Mateur.

Population

The population of the Governorate of Bizerte is approximately 568 219. The majority of the population resides in urban areas, with Bizerte being the most populous city. The people of Bizerte are known for their warm hospitality and rich cultural traditions.

Tourism

Bizerte is a notable destination for tourists in Tunisia. The governorate offers picturesque coastal areas, historical landmarks, and a vibrant cultural scene. Visitors can explore attractions such as the Old Port, Kasbah of Bizerte, and the beautiful beaches along the Mediterranean coast.

Introduction

The Governorate of Kebili is one of the governorates of Tunisia, situated in the southern part of the country. Known for its desert landscapes and oases, Kebili is a region with a unique blend of tradition and natural beauty.

Geography

Kebili covers an area of approximately 22 454 square kilometers and is characterized by its arid terrain. The governorate is known for its oases, including the famous oasis of Tozeur, and its contribution to the Sahara Desert landscape.

Population

The population of the Governorate of Kebili is around 156 961. Kebili is home to a community that has adapted to the challenges of desert life, maintaining cultural traditions and practices that reflect their resilience.

Economy

Kebili's economy is influenced by agriculture, particularly the cultivation of date palms in oases. Tourism also plays a role, with visitors attracted to the unique desert landscapes and cultural experiences offered by the governorate.

Introduction

The Governorate of Sidi Bouzid is one of the governorates of Tunisia, situated in the central part of the country. Known for its agricultural activities and historical significance, Sidi Bouzid is a region with a unique blend of rural and cultural heritage.

Geography

Sidi Bouzid covers an area of approximately 7 400 square kilometers and is characterized by its inland location. The governorate includes both urban and rural areas, contributing to its diverse geographical features.

Economy

Sidi Bouzid's economy is primarily based on agriculture, with a focus on olive and fruit cultivation. The governorate has also played a significant role in the country's history, making it a place of cultural importance.

Agriculture

Agriculture is a key sector in Sidi Bouzid, with the governorate being known for its olive groves and orchards. The fertile land and favorable climate contribute to the success of agricultural activities in the region.
